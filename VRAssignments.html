Assignment 1 - Rotating Banana

This assignment was so complicated, it took me 2 days. We were supposed to animate a rotating banana in Unreal Engine, following a beginner's tutorial we were provided with. It was not even the complexity of the assignment itself, but more my unfamilirity with Unreal Engine as it does not look like any programme I have worked with in the past. I have also made some tiny mistakes throughout and it took me a long time to fix those as I was (and still am) a newbie. Anyway, here is the result of my efforts. In the end, I made a banana turn. Assignment 2 - The Banana Game

This assignment went a little bit smoother than the first one. We animated a rotating banana, now it was time to create a game, where the player has to collect bananas in the playing field. We were supposed to do this in Unreal Engine, following another tutorial we were given a link to. I had a better idea of where to search for Nodes, Meshes, how to compile and how the whole software works in general. I really liked that the outcome was an actual game! So unreal, right? I have again made several mistakes in te process that I needed to fix, too and got frustrated so many times. But I felt very accomplished in the end, while I was collecting the bananas in the game I created myself. However, I have to admit that Unreal Engine pushed my boundaries multiple times during working on the assignments.

Assignment 3 - Projection Mapping

The projection mapping assignment was to use MadMapper, a projection software to project multiple layers of images or video onto an uneven, layered surface.

My projection mapping project was creating an interactive mirror, which would reflect the image from my webcam onto the face-figure and the wall. People are having a lot of fun with it, so I was happy, Although it consists of only 2 layers, the layers were pretty hard to fit onto the mannequine. Everyone around me tried reflecting some kind of artsy patterns onto the objects and I wanted to do it differently, therefore I used the webcam for a real-life input. It was very hard to film in with my phone because of the light emmited by the projector in a dark room, so I am sorry for the video quality.
